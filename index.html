Projects

On-Chip Mechanical Investigation of Joint Mechanotransduction

Fall 2025 - Present

Built a mechanically tunable joint-on-chip system to advance osteoarthritis research and enable human-relevant drug testing. Addressing the $140B annual burden of OA in the US, this platform overcomes limitations of existing models by replicating the complex mechanical environment of human joints—something 2D cultures and animal models cannot achieve. Through iterative design, microfabrication, and quantitative validation, established a reproducible platform for investigating cartilage-bone crosstalk under controlled mechanical stimulation, positioning future personalized medicine approaches for joint disease.

Development of a Head Quantitative Biomimetic Phantom: Accurate MRI Representation of the Brain in Health

Fall 2025 - Present

Served as Test & Systems Engineer on a 6-person engineering team developing a quantitative MRI brain phantom in collaboration with CaliberMRI to standardize clinical imaging calibration. Designed and executed comprehensive verification testing program to validate phantom performance against stakeholder specifications. Developed three critical test protocols: spectrophotometric diffusion analysis, mechanical compression testing, and bubble-leak sealing evaluation. Analyzed experimental data demonstrating 300 µm SLA-printed barriers achieved <1% solute transfer over 48 hours, walls withstood assembly loads with required safety margins, and sealing system maintained hermetic integrity under vacuum conditions.

Test Engineering Spectrophotometry Mechanical Testing Data Analysis Design Verification Protocol Development Medical Devices Systems Integration
